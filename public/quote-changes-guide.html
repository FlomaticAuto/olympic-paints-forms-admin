The Quote & Price Change Log

Every time a rep asks you to redo a quote, change a price, or fix a pricing mistake, log it here. It takes about ten seconds. You are not filing a complaint — you are keeping a simple record so we can finally see the pattern and sort it out.

01 When to log an entry

Log it
  • A rep asks for a brand-new quote
  • A rep wants a quote changed again
  • A rep asks to change the price list
  • You catch or fix a wrong price
  • The same quote comes back a third, fourth, fifth time
Skip it
  • A normal order going through with no price change
  • A general question that isn't about a quote or price
  • Something you already logged five minutes ago — one entry per change
Rule of thumb: if a rep made you do the same work twice, or made you change a price, that's one entry. When in doubt, log it — an extra entry is harmless.

02 How to log one

Open the form
On the tablet or PC, open Quote & Price Change Log. Pick your name — Aziza or Emeshnee — at the top. It remembers you next time.
Tap the rep & what happened
Tap the rep, then the event type (New Quote, Quote Revision, Price-List Change, or Pricing Error). One tap each — no typing.
Add the account & the reason
Type the customer name, then tap the reason (see the list below). If it's a quote being redone, set the revision number — 2 means it's the second time, 3 the third, and so on. That number is what shows how bad the churn is.
Log it
Tap Log Change. Done. If the next entry is for the same rep, tap Log Another (same rep) to save time.

03 Picking the right reason

The reason is the most important part — it's what turns a pile of entries into a clear story. Pick the closest one:

ReasonUse it when…
Rep changed priceThe rep decided to change what they'd already quoted.
Rep changed quantity / productThe rep swapped the sizes, colours, or products on the quote.
Customer renegotiatedThe customer pushed back, so the quote had to change.
Price list out of dateThe price list itself was wrong or old and needs updating.
Rep quoted wrong priceThe rep gave a price that didn't match the price list.
Discount approval neededThe change is waiting on a discount sign-off.
Duplicate / re-requestThe rep asked for the same thing again that was already done.
OtherNone of the above — add a quick note so we know what it was.
Why we're doing this

Right now the constant quote and price changes create a lot of extra admin, and that's where pricing mistakes creep in. Once we have a few weeks of these entries, management can see exactly which reps, which reasons, and how often — and fix the actual cause instead of guessing. Your entries are the evidence. Accurate beats fancy: a quick, honest tap is all we need.
